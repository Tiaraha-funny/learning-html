Nesting rules

This rule is based on using tags and names of the element properly because you are going to nest them in your document.

In your document, you must have these:

  • Syntax:

    You need to use syntax which are starting tags and closing tags. Every single one of them must have an element.

  • Content:

    You need to put the content between the open and the close tags . Content means everything inside or in the middle of the tags.

Lists of the rules

  1. Define a <!DOCTYPE.html> at the top of the document.

  2. You can not have an end tag without an open tag.

  3. Quotation: Do not use quotation inside quotation.

  4. You should use lowercase for element.

  5. DO NOT DO:

    - Never use <ol> as a child of <ul>

    -Never use <p> as a child of <ol> or <ul>

    - Never use <artictle> without <header>

  6. DO THIS INSTEAD:

    - Use <li> as a child of <ol> or <ul> and then put <p> inside.

    - Use <h1> - <h6> as a child <article>

Summary:

Nesting rules are the desciplines of the way how you arrange the tags properly. It allows you to nest your tags properly.
